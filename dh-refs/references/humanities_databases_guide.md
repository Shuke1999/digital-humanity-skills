# Humanities Databases Guide — DH Research

Reference for where to search for secondary literature in digital humanities and related disciplines.

---

## Primary DH Databases

### JSTOR
**URL:** jstor.org  
**Coverage:** Literature, history, art history, philosophy, film studies, area studies. Strong humanities backfile.  
**Best for:** Finding established scholarly articles in literary and historical disciplines.  
**Tip:** Use JSTOR's Text Analyzer to find articles related to a passage of text.

### Project MUSE
**URL:** muse.jhu.edu  
**Coverage:** Humanities and social sciences journals. Published by Johns Hopkins UP.  
**Best for:** Literature, cultural studies, media studies, post-colonial studies, area studies.

### MLA International Bibliography
**URL:** mla.org/Publications/MLA-International-Bibliography  
**Coverage:** Modern languages, linguistics, literary criticism, folklore, film.  
**Best for:** Literary studies, language studies. Most comprehensive for literary scholarship.

### AHCI (Arts & Humanities Citation Index)
**URL:** via Web of Science  
**Coverage:** Cross-disciplinary humanities. Strong citation tracking.  
**Best for:** Finding what cites a given work; tracing influence in the field.

### Historical Abstracts
**URL:** via EBSCO  
**Coverage:** World history (excluding US and Canada history).  
**Best for:** Historical scholarship on global topics.

### America: History and Life
**URL:** via EBSCO  
**Coverage:** US and Canadian history.  
**Best for:** American history scholarship.

## Computational DH Databases

### ACM Digital Library
**URL:** dl.acm.org  
**Coverage:** Computer science, HCI, information systems.  
**Best for:** NLP methods, text analysis tools, human-computer interaction, information retrieval.  
**Key venue:** CHI (Conference on Human Factors), CSCW, SIGIR, DH proceedings via ACM.

### arXiv
**URL:** arxiv.org (cs.CL, cs.IR, cs.DL, cs.AI sections)  
**Coverage:** Pre-prints in NLP, information retrieval, machine learning.  
**Best for:** Very recent computational methods; NLP papers before journal publication.  
**Caveat:** Pre-prints are not peer-reviewed; always check if a published version exists.

### IEEE Xplore
**URL:** ieeexplore.ieee.org  
**Coverage:** Electrical engineering, information technology, signal processing.  
**Best for:** Image processing for historical documents, OCR research.

### Semantic Scholar
**URL:** semanticscholar.org  
**Coverage:** Cross-disciplinary; strong in NLP and computer science.  
**Best for:** Citation graph navigation, finding papers that cite foundational DH methods work.

## Digital Archive & Historical Databases

### HathiTrust Digital Library
**URL:** hathitrust.org  
**Coverage:** 17 million+ digitized volumes; strong for 19th–20th century English-language texts.  
**Best for:** Full-text access to out-of-copyright books; corpus building.  
**DH note:** HathiTrust Research Center (HTRC) provides computational access to the corpus.

### Internet Archive
**URL:** archive.org  
**Coverage:** Books, newspapers, websites, audio, video.  
**Best for:** Early 20th century American newspapers and publications; web archiving.

### Europeana
**URL:** europeana.eu  
**Coverage:** Digitized cultural heritage from European institutions: books, images, maps, music.  
**Best for:** European historical materials; multilingual corpus.

### Digital Public Library of America (DPLA)
**URL:** dp.la  
**Coverage:** Aggregates US libraries, archives, museums.  
**Best for:** American cultural heritage materials; photographs, maps, manuscripts.

### Chronicling America
**URL:** chroniclingamerica.loc.gov  
**Coverage:** Historic American newspapers, 1770–1963, digitized by Library of Congress.  
**Best for:** 19th–early 20th century US newspaper research; OCR quality varies by date/paper.

### Early English Books Online (EEBO)
**URL:** via ProQuest (institutional access)  
**Coverage:** English-language books printed 1473–1700 (STC and Wing periods).  
**Best for:** Early modern English texts; EEBO-TCP provides full-text XML versions.

### Eighteenth Century Collections Online (ECCO)
**URL:** via Gale (institutional access)  
**Coverage:** English-language books, 1701–1800.

### Gallica
**URL:** gallica.bnf.fr  
**Coverage:** French national library digital collections; 6+ million documents.  
**Best for:** French historical materials.

## Specialized DH Repositories

### Zenodo
**URL:** zenodo.org  
**Coverage:** Open-access research outputs including datasets, code, pre-prints.  
**Best for:** Finding DH project datasets and corpora with DOIs.

### DH Commons
**URL:** dhcommons.org / now merged into ADHO infrastructure  
**Coverage:** DH project directory.  
**Best for:** Finding existing DH projects to cite as comparable work.

### GitHub
**URL:** github.com  
**Coverage:** Code and project repositories.  
**Best for:** DH tools, scripts, corpora with version control; cite with DOI via Zenodo archiving.

### Text Encoding Initiative (TEI) Guidelines
**URL:** tei-c.org/guidelines  
**Coverage:** TEI P5 specification and documentation.  
**Best for:** Encoding decision documentation and citation.

## Search Strategy Tips

1. **Combine database types**: For a DH project on 19th-century novels and NLP, search JSTOR (literary criticism) + ACM DL (NLP methods) + HathiTrust (corpus) independently.

2. **Use cited-by tracking**: Find a seminal paper, then use AHCI or Semantic Scholar to find works that cite it — often the fastest path to current scholarship.

3. **Search DH conference proceedings**: ADHO Digital Humanities conference abstracts are available at dh-abstracts.org and reveal cutting-edge work 1–2 years before journal publication.

4. **Check the Bibliography of the History of Art (BHA)** for art history and visual culture DH projects.

5. **For non-Anglophone sources**: Use Redalyc (Latin America), CNKI (China), CiNii (Japan), Cairn (France/Belgium/Switzerland) for regional scholarship.
